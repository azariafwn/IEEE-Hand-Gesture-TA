As observed in Fig. \ref{fig:training_accuracy}, the model achieved rapid convergence. Transient fluctuations observed in the validation metrics, most notably the sharp dip and rapid recovery around epoch 15, are characteristic artifacts of the aggressive 50\% Dropout regularization. These fluctuations confirm that the network maintains stochastic resilience, preventing it from memorizing the training data sequence and ensuring reliable performance on unseen validation samples.

\subsection{Edge Computational Efficiency}

Deploying deep learning architectures directly onto edge devices like the Raspberry Pi 5 requires strict memory and computational management. To quantify the impact of the post-training optimization discussed in Section III-D, a comparative benchmark was conducted between the baseline 32-bit Keras model and the deployed 8-bit quantized TFLite model. 

The evaluation metrics focused on storage footprint, runtime memory (RAM) allocation, and overall classification accuracy. The results of this benchmark are summarized in Table \ref{tab:optimization_comparison}.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\caption{Comparison of Model Resources and Performance: Baseline vs. Optimized}
\label{tab:optimization_comparison}
\centering
\begin{tabular}{l c c c}
\hline
\hline 
\textbf{Metric} & \textbf{Keras (FP32)} & \textbf{TFLite (INT8)} & \textbf{Impact} \\
\hline
Model Size & 2252 KB & 233 KB & \textbf{89.7\% Reduction} \\
RAM Usage & $\approx$ 47.22 MB & $\approx$ 1.07 MB & \textbf{97.7\% Reduction} \\
Accuracy & 98.75\% & 98.50\% & -0.25\% Difference \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The empirical data demonstrates the sheer efficacy of INT8 quantization for edge deployments. The optimization process successfully compressed the physical model size from 2252 KB down to merely 233 KB, achieving an 89.7\% reduction. More importantly for continuous execution, the runtime RAM usage plummeted by 97.7\%, dropping from approximately 47.22 MB to a highly efficient 1.07 MB. 

Crucially, this massive computational relief was achieved without severely compromising the model's predictive capabilities. The quantized TFLite model experienced a negligible accuracy degradation of only 0.25\% compared to the baseline FP32 model. This proves that the system is exceptionally lightweight, leaving ample system memory for the Raspberry Pi to concurrently run the MediaPipe extraction pipeline and State Machine logic without risk of memory overflow or thermal throttling.


\subsection{Sequential Interaction Performance}

The ultimate viability of a smart home control system relies on its real-time responsiveness and sequential logic accuracy, extending beyond static testing. The proposed system was subjected to a comprehensive live evaluation on the Raspberry Pi 5 to assess both its computational speed and the robustness of the State Machine in handling sequential commands, such as combining a Selection Gesture with an Action Gesture.

The evaluation of real-time sequential interactions requires a clear mapping between the physical gesture sequences recognized by the LSTM model and the final logical commands executed by the State Machine. Each command sequence is composed of two distinct phases: an initial Action Gesture followed by a specific Selection Gesture. Table \ref{tab:logical_mapping} details the specific combinations used to trigger the eight smart home commands. For example, the logical command \texttt{D1\_ON} is achieved by executing the \texttt{close\_to\_open\_palm} gesture (Action) immediately followed by the \texttt{open\_to\_one} gesture (Selection). This deterministic logic ensures that device activation is intentional and mediated by the integrated State Machine. Operational reliability was subsequently evaluated by testing these 8 distinct logical command combinations in real-time.

The overall system remains highly robust for daily smart home interactions despite these minor anomalies, as evidenced by the consistently high precision and recall values.

\subsection{Environmental Robustness}

The final evaluation phase assesses the system's reliability across varying environmental conditions, specifically focusing on distance, lighting intensity, and camera resolution. These tests validate the robustness of the MediaPipe-based landmark extraction and the generalization capabilities of the LSTM model.

\subsubsection{Distance Variation}
The system was tested at three distinct distances: 30 cm (Close), 50 cm (Ideal), and 70 cm (Far), with 90 trials performed for each gesture class per variation. As shown in Table \ref{tab:robustness_distance}, the model maintained high accuracy across all ranges, achieving a peak mean accuracy of 99.6\% at the ideal distance of 50 cm. Even at 70 cm, the accuracy remained robust at 98.6\%. This consistency confirms that the geometric normalization of hand landmarks relative to the wrist effectively mitigates the impact of hand-scale variations caused by distance changes.
